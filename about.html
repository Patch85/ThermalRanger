Thermal Ranger: What is it?

Thermal Ranger allows for the active monitoring of temperatures during primary and secondary fermentation of wort. An acceptable range of temperatures is defined, based on the desired fermentation profile of a particular beer style being brewed. Typically, fermentation of ales takes place between 68 and 72 degrees fahrenheit, while lager fermentation generally takes place beteen 45 and 55 degrees fahrenheit.

If the fermentation temperature drifts outside the acceptable range, a text notification is sent to the user's phone. This allows the homebrewer to take any necessary steps in adjusting ambient temperature of the fermentation environment, in order to bring the wort back into the desired temperature range.
